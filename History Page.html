MODERN PALESTINE MAPS

Palestine,[i] officially the State of Palestine,[ii][e] is a country in the Southern Levant region of West Asia recognized by 146 out of
193 UN member states. It encompasses the Israeli-occupied West Bank, including East Jerusalem, and the Gaza Strip, collectively known as
the occupied Palestinian territories, within the broader geographic and historical Palestine region. Palestine shares most of its borders
with Israel, and it borders Jordan to the east and Egypt to the southwest. It has a total land area of 6,020 square kilometres (2,320 sq mi)
while its population exceeds five million people. Its proclaimed capital is Jerusalem, while Ramallah serves as its administrative center.
Gaza City was its largest city prior to evacuations in 2023.



HISTORY OF PALESTINE

3000 BCE - 586 BCE
Ancient History
3000 BCE – Canaanites establish city-states.
1000 BCE – Kingdoms of Israel & Judah emerge.
586 BCE – Babylonian conquest, Jewish exile.
539 BCE - 135 CE
Persian, Greek & Roman Rule
539 BCE – Persians allow Jewish return.
332 BCE – Alexander the Great conquers Palestine.
135 CE – Romans rename it "Palestina" after suppressing Jewish revolts.
638 CE - 1516
Islamic Rule
638 CE – Muslims conquer Palestine.
1187 CE – Saladin retakes Jerusalem.
1516 – Ottoman Empire rules for 400 years.
1917 - 1947
British Rule & Zionism
1917 – Balfour Declaration supports a Jewish homeland.
1920–1947 – Jewish migration increases under British Mandate.
1947 – UN proposes partition; tensions rise.
1948 - Today
Nakba & Modern Conflict
1948 – Nakba: 750,000 Palestinians displaced.
1967 – Israel occupies Gaza, West Bank, & East Jerusalem.
1987 – First Intifada uprising.
1993 – Oslo Accords attempt peace but fail.
2000 – Second Intifada, more occupation.
Today – Ongoing struggle for Palestinian self-determination.
